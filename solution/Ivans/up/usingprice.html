Is an electric car expensive or normal?

The expert calculated the costs

The approximate economics of owning an electric car include costs for “fuel”, maintenance and replacement of consumable parts (brakes, rims, tires, etc.)

The process has started

Until now, we've looked at electric cars mostly as expensive toys for eccentric weirdos. The perspective is changing: by 2030, there will be 150 thousand charging stations in the world (20 thousand by 2024), and the share of sales of electric vehicles should increase from today’s 0.1% to 15%.

This is not just another project invented to absorb billions, but a necessary measure. The global trend is that soon everyone will have to pay for their carbon footprint. Therefore, Europe decided to abandon internal combustion engines and produce only electric vehicles.

The good and the bad

The purchase of electric cars in Latvia is already being stimulated - with a date for the purchase of new cars (4,500 euros) and free parking.

Minuses

The main drawback was and will remain the long charging time.

Maintenance costs

Experts from the US Department of Energy have calculated that the cost of servicing electric cars is 40% lower. Due to what?

Let's compare the expenses for the Mercedes-Benz EQC electric car and the Mercedes-Benz GLC gasoline car per 100 kilometers, taking into account all the specified costs and the cost of charging or refueling:

**Expenses for the Mercedes-Benz EQC electric car per 100 km:**

  • Charging: 43 euros per 100 kWh
  • Electricity consumption: 8 euros per 100 km
  • Winter tires: 82 euros
  • Brake discs: 93 euros
  • Filter replacement: 25 euros

**Expenses for the Mercedes-Benz GLC gasoline car per 100 km:**

  • Refueling: 155 euros per full tank. Gasoline consumption: 18 euros per 100 km
  • Oil change: 4 euros
  • Winter tires: 90 euros
  • Spark plug replacement: 15 euros
  • Antifreeze replacement: 11 euros
  • Filter replacement: 35 euros
